Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Last Updated: October 23, 2025

Summary: OPAL X is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information in accordance with Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

1. Introduction

OPAL X ("we," "our," or "us") operates websites and provides digital services to Australian businesses. We are committed to protecting and respecting your privacy in compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, business address
  • Business Information: Company name, ABN/ACN, industry, website URL
  • Technical Information: IP address, browser type, device information, cookies
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, navigation patterns, referral sources
  • Communications: Emails, chat messages, form submissions, support tickets

2.2 How We Collect Information

We collect information through:

  • Website forms and contact pages
  • Email and phone communications
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies
  • Analytics tools (Google Analytics 4, Microsoft Clarity)
  • CRM systems (HubSpot, Pipedrive)
  • Third-party integrations and APIs

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide Services: Deliver web design, development, SEO, and digital marketing services
  • Communication: Respond to inquiries, send updates, provide customer support
  • Service Improvement: Analyze website performance, optimize user experience
  • Marketing: Send newsletters, promotional content (with consent)
  • Compliance: Meet legal obligations, prevent fraud, enforce terms
  • Analytics: Track conversions, measure ROI, generate reports

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information with:

  • Service Providers: Hosting providers, CDN services, email platforms, payment processors
  • Analytics Partners: Google Analytics, Microsoft Clarity, Meta Pixel
  • Marketing Tools: MailChimp, HubSpot, LinkedIn Ads
  • Legal Requirements: When required by Australian law or court order
  • Business Transfers: In the event of merger, acquisition, or asset sale

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

5. International Data Transfers

OPAL X operates in Australia with development offices in Sri Lanka. Your data may be transferred to and processed in Sri Lanka under our direct supervision. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information in accordance with Australian privacy standards.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Secure cloud hosting with Australian CDN POPs
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF) protection
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Access controls and two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Regular backups and disaster recovery plans

7. Your Privacy Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request access to your personal information
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal obligations)
  • Opt-Out: Unsubscribe from marketing communications
  • Complaint: Lodge a complaint with us or the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)

8. Cookie Policy

We use cookies for analytics, personalization, and marketing. You can control cookies through your browser settings. See our Cookie Policy for more details.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. Continued use of our services constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and tracking technologies

Last Updated: October 23, 2025

What are cookies? Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us improve your experience, analyze usage, and deliver personalized content.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They enable websites to remember your preferences, analyze traffic, and provide personalized experiences.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

Purpose: Required for website functionality

  • Session management
  • Security and authentication
  • Load balancing
  • Cookie consent preferences

Duration: Session or up to 1 year

Legal Basis: Legitimate interest (website functionality)

2.2 Analytics Cookies

Purpose: Understand how visitors use our website

  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4): Page views, user behavior, traffic sources
  • Microsoft Clarity: Heatmaps, session recordings, user interactions
  • Google Tag Manager (GTM): Tag management and event tracking
  • Google Search Console: Search performance and indexing data

Duration: Up to 1 year

Legal Basis: Consent

3. Third-Party Cookies

We use third-party services that may set their own cookies:

  • Google LLC: Analytics, Ads, Tag Manager, Fonts
  • Microsoft Corporation: Clarity analytics
  • Meta Platforms: Facebook Pixel
  • LinkedIn Corporation: Insight Tag
  • Cloudflare: CDN and security
  • HubSpot: CRM and marketing automation

These third parties have their own privacy policies. We recommend reviewing them.

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Improve Performance: Analyze website speed, identify errors, optimize user experience
  • Understand Behavior: Track navigation patterns, popular content, conversion funnels
  • Measure ROI: Track campaign performance, attribute conversions, calculate cost-per-lead
  • Personalize Content: Remember preferences, customize experiences, serve relevant content
  • Security: Prevent fraud, detect suspicious activity, protect user accounts
  • Marketing: Retarget visitors, optimize ad campaigns, measure ad effectiveness

5. Cookie Duration

5.1 Session Cookies

Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser. Used for essential functionality.

5.2 Persistent Cookies

Remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted. Used for analytics and marketing.

  • Short-term: Up to 30 days (campaign tracking)
  • Medium-term: Up to 1 year (preferences, functional)
  • Long-term: Up to 2 years (analytics, user identification)

6. Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

We use GA4 with Google Consent Mode v2 to:

  • Measure website traffic and user behavior
  • Understand audience demographics (age, gender, interests)
  • Track conversions and goal completions
  • Generate performance reports and insights

Data Collected: Page views, events, user properties, device information, geographic location (city-level)

Data Retention: 14 months (configurable)

IP Anonymization: Enabled

7. Microsoft Clarity

We use Microsoft Clarity to:

  • View heatmaps of user clicks and scrolling
  • Watch session recordings (with sensitive data masked)
  • Identify usability issues and friction points
  • Optimize conversion rates

Privacy: Personal information (emails, passwords, credit card numbers) is automatically masked in recordings.

8. Consent Management

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept All: Allow all cookies including analytics and marketing
  • Reject Non-Essential: Allow only essential cookies
  • Customize: Choose which cookie categories to enable

You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our footer.

9. Managing Cookies

9.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

9.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Google Ads: Ads Settings
  • Facebook: Ad Preferences
  • LinkedIn: Advertising Preferences

9.3 Do Not Track (DNT)

While we respect DNT signals, most analytics and advertising platforms do not respond to DNT. We recommend using the opt-out tools above for more effective control.

10. Impact of Blocking Cookies

Blocking or deleting cookies may affect website functionality:

  • Preferences may not be saved
  • Some features may not work properly
  • Login sessions may not persist
  • Forms may not auto-fill
  • Performance may be reduced

Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for core functionality.

11. Server-Side Tagging

For select clients, we offer server-side tagging through Google Tag Manager Server-Side. This approach:

  • Improves page load speed
  • Enhances data privacy and control
  • Reduces reliance on client-side cookies
  • Provides more accurate tracking

12. Mobile Apps

Our mobile apps may use similar technologies including:

  • Device identifiers (IDFA, Android Advertising ID)
  • Local storage
  • SDKs (Software Development Kits)
  • Push notification tokens

You can control these through your device settings.

13. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in technology, legal requirements, or our practices. Check this page periodically for updates.

14. Your Rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and Australian Privacy Principles, you have rights regarding cookies and tracking:

  • Right to Object: Opt out of non-essential cookies
  • Right to Access: Request information about data collected via cookies
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of cookie data
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Change cookie preferences at any time
